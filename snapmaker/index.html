Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Artisan worth the extra money over the 2.0?

Yes, if you can swing it. The Artisan has a bigger build volume (400mm vs 350mm), dual extrusion, a 40W laser (vs 10W stock on 2.0), industrial-grade linear rails, and a proper enclosure. The quick-swap system is also much faster. The 2.0 is great value, but the Artisan is the better machine.

Can I upgrade my 2.0 to the newer modules?

Partially. Snapmaker sells upgrade kits for the 2.0 including the 20W/40W laser with Air Assist, 200W CNC module, and Quick Swap Kit. But you can't upgrade to Artisan-level linear rails or the U1's toolhead system. At some point, a new machine makes more sense than stacking upgrades.

What's the difference between the J1 and J1s?

The J1s adds a print cooling fan (you no longer need to remove the top panel for PLA), includes breakaway support filament in the box, and comes with doors and side panels pre-installed. If you're buying new, get the J1s.

Does the U1 do laser or CNC?

No — the U1 is a dedicated 3D printer. It trades the 3-in-1 versatility for much faster print speeds and the 4-toolhead color system. If you need laser or CNC, go Artisan or 2.0.

How loud are these machines?

The Artisan and U1 have enclosed chambers that dampen noise significantly — quiet enough for a home office. The 2.0 is louder since it doesn't have a full enclosure (though you can add one). CNC carving is naturally louder than printing, regardless of model.
